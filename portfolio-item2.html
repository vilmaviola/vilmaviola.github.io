Gender perception
of humanoid robot

Human Perception

Pepper is a social humanoid robot, made to interact with humans through speech and motions. It is able to recognize speech, basic emotions and even faces. According to the manufacturer Softbank Robotics, Pepper is a genderless robot. That is, Pepper’s physical appearance and voice are designed to be neither male nor female.

This study aimed to investigate whether or not, and how, the perception of Pepper’s social gender is affected by feminine and masculine characteristics in Pepper’s voice and upper body movements.


We programmed three different movements using the software Choreographe (Version 2.5.10.7). In addition to the different body movements, three sounds of computer-generated speech were generated. The sentence spoken was “Did you know that Venus is the only planet that spins clockwise?” The sentence was chosen since it’s not typically assigned to male or female habits and stereotypes, and also that it didn’t express a human or a robot need. Two of the sounds were created using Google’s Text-to-Speech API and the third one used the Text-to-Speech voice that was generated through Choreograph.


A study was then conducted to investigate a humans' perception of the humanoid robot’s gender and if the movements or the voice characteristics influenced that perception.
